Native Gardens





Opened in 1965, the Western Australian Native Garden features a scientific collection mainly of Western Australian plants displayed en masse or as individual specimens in an inspiring landscape.The Native Garden is unique from those around the world by different reasons.

  • Focus on local flora
  • Extraordinary diversity of Western Australian wildflowers
  • 2,000 of Western Australia's 12,000 species of plants
  • Regional floras of great species richness and beauty
  • Favourite for weddings, quiet picnics or resting in the sun
 

Duke University, Perth





Duke Gardens provides a place where people of all backgrounds and ages come for beauty, education, horticulture, solitude, discovery, study, renewal and inspiration. Often spoken of as "the crown jewel of Duke University," Duke Gardens occupies 55 acres in the heart of west campus, adjacent to Duke University Medical Center.

  • One of the premier public gardens in Australia
  • Renowned for landscape design and the quality of horticulture
  • Over 40,000 indigenous trees from seed collected on the property
  • Stone from an old quarry used for low walls and gateways
  • Main entry drive lined with double rows of poplars
 

Queens Park





Queens Park and Botanic Garden covers approximately 400 hectares, including 267 hectares of significant remnant bushland. The escarpment rises steeply from the Swan River to a height of 65 m and, as a high point in the landscape, is visible from the city foreshore and surrounding suburbs.

  • Overlook Perth city, the Swan River and Darling Scarp
  • Limestone cliffs exposed above Mounts Bay Road
  • 3 plant communities (heathland, woodland and low moist areas)
  • 324 species of local native plants in the bushland
  • Over 70 bird species, 20 reptile species, hundreds of invertebrates
  • Rare population and habitat of native snail, Bothriembryon indutus
